My story

Not your average new physio.

Most physiotherapists learn about the body in a classroom. I learned it the hard way first — through ten years of training clients in Bedford and a professional boxing career where getting injured, rehabbing, and coming back stronger was simply part of the job.

I took a sports science degree into a decade of one-to-one coaching at Lovell Personal Training: office workers with wrecked backs, runners with stubborn knees, people recovering from surgery, and fighters preparing for the hardest nights of their lives.

Physiotherapy wasn't a career change. It was the obvious next step — putting formal clinical training on top of ten years of real-world experience. I graduated from the University of Northampton with first-class honours, and I treat every patient with the same standard I trained athletes to: no guesswork, no endless appointments, a clear plan and a finish line.

FAQ

Good questions, straight answers.

Do I need a GP referral?

No. You can book directly with me — most people do. If anything in your assessment needs a GP or a scan, I'll tell you and write to them for you.

How many sessions will I need?

Most straightforward problems need somewhere between three and six sessions. You'll get an honest estimate at your initial assessment, and if you're progressing faster than planned, we finish early.

What should I wear?

Something you can move in — gym kit is perfect. If we're assessing a knee or hip, shorts make life easier.

Where do sessions happen?

At my private facility on Meadow Lane, Bedford — the same gym I've trained clients in for ten years. One-to-one, no crowds, free parking.

Can you help if I've had pain for years?

Often, yes. Persistent pain usually needs a different approach, not more of the same. The free call is the right place to start — I'll give you an honest view before you commit to anything.

What if physio isn't the right thing for me?

Then I'll say so, at the free call, and point you towards whoever is — a GP, a consultant, or a different specialist. I'd rather lose a booking than waste your time and money.
